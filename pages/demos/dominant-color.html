Dominant color — placeholder backgrounds before the pixels arrive

Each tile's dimensions arrive in ~150ms via prepare()'s byte probe; the averaged dominant color lands a moment later, once the stream has fully drained. The left panel leaves each tile's background at the page's default grey. The right panel paints the dominant color behind the image the moment it arrives — so an empty tile already evokes the photo about to fill it, and half-loaded scrolling feels less skeletal.
